Case Study — 01

Re-imagining the GDS ‘Good Practice Guides’

Designing end-to-end public services at scale, embedded within GDS across policy, technology, and operations.

01

Background

Embedded within GDS, I led service design across complex, multi-channel government services. Working across policy, technology, and operations to reduce friction for citizens and caseworkers alike.

The challenge was not only to improve individual touchpoints, but to surface the systemic issues that caused inconsistency across the estate — and provide a model for how good practice could travel across departments.

02

Approach

We ran a series of contextual enquiry sessions with caseworkers across three regional offices, shadowing daily workflows and identifying the points at which the designed service diverged from lived reality.

“The most important thing was making the invisible visible — mapping what caseworkers actually did versus what policy intended.”

Service blueprint mapped across five departments, co-created with frontline caseworkers.

Workshop synthesis sessions brought together policy leads, product managers, and frontline staff to pressure-test the emerging recommendations before they were codified into guidance.

03

Outcome

The resulting practice guides were adopted across four government departments and informed the GDS updated guidance on end-to-end service design. A framework for measuring service quality at the seams between departments was piloted with two agencies.
